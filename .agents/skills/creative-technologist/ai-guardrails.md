# Skill: AI Guardrails

## Purpose

Use this skill when designing AI workflows that need quality, safety, brand control, and production discipline.

## Core Principle

AI should speed up the workflow, not remove accountability.

Guardrails are what keep AI systems useful instead of risky, generic, or chaotic.

## Required Guardrails

### Human-in-the-Loop Review

AI can draft, organize, summarize, and suggest. Humans approve.

### No Auto-Publish

Do not create systems that automatically publish AI-generated creative, marketing copy, client-facing content, or sensitive communications without review.

### Source Context Required

For any factual, brand, client, or campaign-specific output, require source context before generation.

### Brand Lock

Use approved brand rules, voice guidance, claims, disclaimers, and examples to prevent generic or off-brand output.

### Risk Flagging

Flag outputs that may require:

- Legal review
- Compliance review
- Brand review
- Strategy review
- Client approval
- Source verification

### Traceable Outputs

Keep outputs tied to:

- Input brief
- Context pack
- Prompt/template
- Reviewer
- Status
- Date/version

### Documentation

Every workflow should explain:

- What the system does
- What AI is used for
- What humans must review
- What the output means
- What should not be trusted blindly

## Red Flags

Stop or escalate when:

- Output includes unsupported claims
- The model invents facts
- Brand rules are missing
- User data is sensitive
- Legal or compliance approval may be required
- The workflow encourages blind trust
- The automation removes necessary human judgment

## Design Standard

Good AI systems make humans faster and better informed. They do not hide risk.
