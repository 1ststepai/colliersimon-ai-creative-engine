# Skill: Creative QA

## Purpose

Use this skill when reviewing AI-generated creative, copy, strategy, documentation, or production outputs.

The goal is to make AI output usable, safe, and aligned with brand, channel, and production requirements.

## Core Principle

AI output is a draft, not a deliverable.

A Creative Technologist should design QA systems that make AI outputs easier to review, approve, reject, improve, or hand off.

## QA Checklist

### Strategy Fit

- Does the output match the brief?
- Does it address the intended audience?
- Does it support the campaign goal?
- Is the message hierarchy clear?
- Is the output useful for the channel?

### Brand Fit

- Does it match the brand voice?
- Does it avoid off-brand language?
- Does it respect approved claims?
- Does it avoid generic AI phrasing?
- Does it feel like it could belong to the brand?

### Production Fit

- Is the format usable?
- Is the output the right length?
- Are naming conventions clear?
- Are the deliverables organized?
- Is the handoff obvious?

### Accuracy and Risk

- Are claims factually supported?
- Are sensitive statements flagged?
- Are legal/compliance items identified?
- Are assumptions clearly marked?
- Is anything overpromising?

### Human Judgment

- Does this need a strategist’s review?
- Does this need a copywriter’s pass?
- Does this need design review?
- Does this need legal or client approval?
- Should this be rejected instead of revised?

## QA Output Statuses

Use these statuses:

- Ready for review
- Needs brand pass
- Needs copy edit
- Needs strategy review
- Needs legal/compliance review
- Needs source verification
- Approved for handoff
- Rejected

## Rule

No AI-generated output should be considered production-ready until it passes human review.
